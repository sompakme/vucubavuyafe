Early Life and Career Beginnings

Tommy Mottola was born on July 14, 1949, in The Bronx, New York. He started his career in the music industry as a member of the band ‘The Exotics.’ However, his real breakthrough came when he transitioned into the business side of music.

Rise to Prominence

Mottola’s rise to prominence began when he founded Champion Entertainment Organization. His keen eye for talent led him to discover and manage several successful artists, including Hall & Oates and Carly Simon. His success caught the attention of CBS Records, which later became Sony Music Entertainment.

Head of Sony Music Entertainment

As the head of Sony Music Entertainment, Mottola was instrumental in the careers of many iconic artists, including Mariah Carey, Celine Dion, and Jennifer Lopez. His leadership saw the company’s revenue double, making it one of the most profitable labels in the world.

Post-Sony Career

After leaving Sony in 2003, Mottola continued to make his mark in the music industry. He founded the Mottola Media Group, a company that focuses on music, entertainment, fashion, and multimedia.

Author and Television Producer

Mottola has also ventured into writing and television production. His memoir, “Hitmaker: The Man and His Music,” was a New York Times bestseller. He has also produced several successful television shows, including “The X Factor.”

Real Estate Investments

Aside from his music ventures, Mottola has made significant investments in real estate. He owns several luxurious properties in New York and Florida, contributing to his substantial net worth.

Philanthropy

Mottola is also known for his philanthropic efforts. He has donated to several charities and causes, including the National Multiple Sclerosis Society and the Robin Hood Foundation.

Recognition and Awards

Over the years, Mottola has received numerous awards for his contributions to the music industry. These include the City of Hope Spirit of Life Award and the Ellis Island Medal of Honor.

Personal Life

Mottola has been married three times. His most notable marriage was to pop superstar Mariah Carey, which ended in divorce. He is currently married to Mexican singer and actress Thalia, with whom he has two children.
