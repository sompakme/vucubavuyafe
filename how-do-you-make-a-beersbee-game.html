What are the Beersbee rules and regulations?

Beersbee Rules are as follows:

Every player is required to stay behind their poles at all times.

Each player/team takes turns attempting to identify the target on the pole that is the farthest away from them on an alternating basis.

The opposing side gets the option to catch the frisbee or to reclaim the target if it is knocked off the pole after it has been hit by the target or the frisbee has been struck by the pole.

What is the origin of the term “Polish horseshoes”?

Polish horseshoes, contrary to its name, do not entail the use of horseshoes and did not originate in the country of Poland. It is really called “Polish horseshoes” since the game is played with a disc rather than horseshoes, and the “Polish” portion of its name is taken from the fact that it requires two poles to be played, and so the game is referred to as being “pole-ish.”
