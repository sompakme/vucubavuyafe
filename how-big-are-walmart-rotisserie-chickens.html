The Great Walmart Rotisserie Chicken: A Quest for the Truth (and Maybe Some Dinner)

Ah, the Walmart rotisserie chicken. A legend, a mystery, a source of endless fascination (and maybe just a touch of hunger) for bargain hunters and busy families alike. But one question has plagued us all: just how big are these things?

The Official Line: According to the Walmart website, their standard rotisserie chicken comes in at a respectable 36 ounces. That's roughly 1 pound and 13 ounces, which is enough to feed a small family or one very enthusiastic individual (no judgment here). However, there's more to the story than meets the eye (or the chicken).

Enter the "Family Size" : Walmart also offers a "Family Size" rotisserie chicken, which clocks in at a hefty 44 ounces. That's almost 2 pounds and 12 ounces of deliciousness, perfect for those nights when you just can't decide between chicken pot pie, a chicken salad sandwich, and, well, just straight-up roasted chicken.

But Is Bigger Always Better? Now, before you go rushing to the nearest Walmart and grabbing the biggest bird you can find, consider this: bigger doesn't always mean more meat. Sometimes, those "family size" chickens are just, well, fluffier. They might look impressive, but they might not actually yield much more edible chicken than their smaller counterparts.

The Verdict: So, the answer to the age-old question is... it depends! If you're feeding a crowd, the "Family Size" might be the way to go. But if you're just looking for a quick and easy meal for one or two, the standard size will do just fine.

Pro Tip: No matter which size you choose, be sure to snag one of those free plastic containers from the deli section. It's the perfect way to transport your precious poultry home without making a mess (and let's be honest, who wants to clean chicken drippings out of their car?).

In conclusion, the quest for the perfect Walmart rotisserie chicken is an ongoing adventure. But hey, at least the journey is delicious!
