Spill the Tea: Unveiling the Cost of Afternoon Delight at The Ivy London

So, you've got your pinky finger at the ready and your appetite for fancy finger foods tingling. You're dreaming of an afternoon spent sipping tea in the iconic surroundings of The Ivy London. But before you don the fascinator and dust off your monocle (optional, but highly encouraged for maximum poshness), there's one burning question: how much does this delightful indulgence set you back?

Here's the Crumpet-Crumbs on the Cost:

The good news, my friend, is that afternoon tea at The Ivy London won't break the bank (unless you accidentally order a whole plate of those delectable macarons – they're irresistible, we warned you!). The price for this quintessential British experience varies depending on whether you fancy a spot of bubbly with your scones:

  • The Classic Carriage Ride: £32.95 per person gets you a delightful selection of finger sandwiches, freshly baked scones with all the trimmings (clotted cream, jam, the works!), and a divine selection of pastries and cakes. Tea, infusions, or coffee are also included, so you can sip to your heart's content.
  • The Champagne Gallop: Feeling fancy? For an extra £10, you can add a glass of bubbly to your afternoon tea, making it £42.95 per person. Because, well, why not? It is a special occasion, after all.

But Wait, There's More!

For those with dietary restrictions, The Ivy offers a variety of options, so don't fret! Just be sure to let them know when you make your reservation.

Pro Tip: Afternoon tea is served daily from 3pm to 4.45pm, so be sure to book your table in advance. These coveted spots tend to disappear faster than a Victoria sponge at a vicar's tea party!

FAQs: Your Afternoon Tea Survival Guide

  • How to Book Afternoon Tea at The Ivy London? Head over to their website or call the specific Ivy location you'd like to visit.
  • How to Dress for Afternoon Tea? Smart casual is the way to go. Think sundresses, dress pants, or a collared shirt. But hey, if you feel like rocking a top hat and tails, we won't judge (we might even be secretly impressed).
  • How Much Time Should I Allow for Afternoon Tea? Plan for around 1.5 to 2 hours to fully enjoy the experience. After all, there's no rush when you're surrounded by fancy finger foods and endless cups of tea!
  • How to Eat a Scone? The proper etiquette is to break the scone in half, then spread on your clotted cream and jam – but hey, if you prefer to devour it whole, we won't tell a soul (we might even join you in that scone-sational act!).
  • How Much to Tip? A 10-12% tip is standard in the UK.

So there you have it! Now that you're armed with the knowledge of afternoon tea costs and etiquette, you're all set for a delightful experience at The Ivy London. Just remember to bring your appetite, your sense of adventure, and maybe a spare napkin (those pastries can get a little messy, in the best way possible). Happy sipping!
