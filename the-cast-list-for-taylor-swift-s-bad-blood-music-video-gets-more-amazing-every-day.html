The cast list for Taylor Swift's Bad Blood music video gets more amazing every day

July 07, 2024
blog

    Over the last six days, Taylor Swift has been teasing her fans as she slowly unveils the cast for her new music video, . Featuring a long, long list of Hollywood's biggest and brightest young stars, fans are now wondering who won't star in the video.

    The reveal began six days ago when, through Instagram, Taylor Swift introduced her fans to Catastrophe, Taylor's alter-ego who is the star of her new music video for .

    She then started to slowly reveal pictures of the others, such as Ellie Goulding as Destructa X, Hailee Steinfeld as The Trinity, and Karlie Kloss as Knockout, and most recently Cara Delevingne as Mother Chucker.

    However the cast list has now grown and includes - Victoria's Secret model Lily Aldridge as Frostbite, Paramore singer Hayley Williams as the Crimson Curse, models Gigi Hadid as Slay-Z, Martha Hunt as Homeslice, and Karlie as Knockout, British singer Ellie Goulding as Destructa X, actresses Lena Dunham as Lucky Fiora, Hailee as The Trinity, Serayah as Dilemma, Jessica Alba as Domino, and Zendaya as Cut-Throat.

    Kendrick Lamar is the only male in the video, starring as Welvin da Great.

    Rumours are still swirling about who else may star in the music video, with Cara Delevingne and Selena Gomez both still missing from the Instagram reveal.

    Fans had also been circulating via social media a rumoured cast list which threw up some surprising names - including Ellen Pompeo and Mariska Hargitay - and Taylor all but confirmed the existence of the list when she shared a snap on Thursday of Ellen as Luna and Mariska as Justice.

    The two actresses are known to Taylor's fans as Meredith Grey in and Olivia Benson in , two strong female characters whom Taylor named her cats after.

    Taylor also confirmed on Twitter that each cast member chose their own character name, writing: "Fun fact about #BadBloodMusicVideo: Each individual actor/actress chose their character's name and persona. ?CATastrophe?*"

    A short 56-second teaser video was also released but offered no clues to fans, who have so far deduced that due to the style of the images, the video is a-style take on friendships.

    The song is famously about the rift between Taylor and fellow pop star Katy Perry.
