John Donahoe II (born April 30, 1960) is the president and chief executive officer of eBay, a publicly traded company (since March 31, 2008).

When he was recruited as president of eBay Marketplaces, it was in March of 200His responsibilities included concentrating on eBay’s core business, which contributes for a significant portion of the company’s sales. In all, he has completed more than forty acquisitions, including Shopping.com and StubHub, as well as the classifieds websites Gumtree.com and LoQUo, and the most recent, Shutl. Mr. Donahoe is now responsible for overseeing eBay’s two modules, eBay Marketplaces and PayPal, in his present position. 

Previously, he spent 20 years working for Bain & Company (a global consulting company headquartered in Boston), where he began as an associate consultant and rose through the ranks to become the firm’s president and chief executive officer. Mr. Donahoe’s first job out of high school came as a result of a friend’s father inviting him to work at his Schlitz beer distribution business after the completion of his senior year of high school. “You’ll earn a lot of money,” he assured her. His employer required that he become a member of the International Brotherhood of Teamsters before he could begin working.
