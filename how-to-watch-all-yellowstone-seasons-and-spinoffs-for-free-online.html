After four seasons of fighting off billionaire land developers, politicians and wolves in Big Sky Country, Yellowstone returned last fall with the first part of the fifth and final season — only to leave fans on the edge of their seats with no premiere date for the final episodes. At long last, Dutton Ranch superfans finally have good news: Paramount has announced that the last part of Yellowstone season five will return in 2024, and two more spinoffs are in the works.

Those who want to stream all current episodes of Yellowstone online without cable can finally watch them all in one place: Peacock. (The streamer now has the first four complete seasons and part one, season five of the series.)

You can also buy or rent every season or individual episodes on Amazon Prime Video, Apple TV and other video-on-demand platforms. Re-runs of Paramount Network’s SAG-nominated drama are currently airing on CBS on Sunday nights to fill out the writers’ strike-affected primetime schedule.

The final part of season five was originally scheduled to premiere this year, but production went on hiatus due to the writers strike and a dispute with Golden Globe-winning star Kevin Costner.

A present-day sequel series with the working title of 2024 and another prequel, 1944, will continue the Dutton family tree saga, joining Yellowstone creator Taylor Sheridan‘s existing prequels, 1883 and 1923. (Both are currently streaming on Paramount+, where another spinoff, the oil drama 6666, will eventually land.)

In the meantime, there are plenty of ways for fans and new viewers to catch up on the entire Dutton family tree on cable and streaming. Keep reading below for all of the ways to stream past and current seasons of Yellowstone and its spinoff series online without cable, including for free.

Yellowstone Season 5: Premiere Date and Time, Episode Release Schedule

Part two of Yellowstone season five will begin airing in November 2024 on Paramount Network. An exact number of episodes has not been announced, and Sheridan previously told THR that the final part could span more than the originally planned six episodes. “If I think it takes 10 episodes to wrap it up, they’ll give me 10. It’ll be as long as it needs to be,” he said.

How to Watch Yellowstone Season 5 Online With Cable

When Yellowstone season five finally returns on Paramount Network, you’ll need a subscription to traditional cable or to a live TV streaming service that carries the network. At the moment, the only way to watch Yellowstone on cable is when re-runs air live on Paramount Network or CBS, which are available on traditional cable or on select online TV streaming services. Those with cable can log in with their TV provider credentials at paramountnetwork.com to watch the channel online live.

How to Watch Yellowstone Season 5 Online Without Cable

If you want to cut the cord, DirecTV Stream ($64.99 and up monthly) and Hulu + Live TV ($76.99 and up per month) are among the best live TV streaming services for watching Yellowstone on Paramount Network without cable.

When the second part of Yellowstone season five does finally arrive, those who pre-ordered the season Prime Video, Apple TV and other streamers will be able to watch the new episodes the day after they air on Paramount Network.

New subscribers of Fubo ($74.99 and up monthly) and Philo ($25 and up monthly) — which both offer free seven-day trials — can watch the first episode of Yellowstone season five for free when they sign up for either service before the series premieres. Sling ($40-$60 per month, plus a $6 add-on fee for Paramount Network and other channels), is giving new customers half off their first month of service.

What Is Yellowstone About?

Yellowstone follows the Dutton family, helmed by patriarch John Dutton (Kevin Costner), as they protect their cattle ranch in Montana against land developers and others at its borders, including America’s first National Park and a Native American reservation. The show is the last scripted original series remaining on Paramount Network, where it debuted in 2018. It also stars Luke Grimes, Kelly Reilly, Wes Bentley, Cole Hauser, Kesley Asbille, Brecken Merrill and others.

The hit show and its prequels have set TV ratings records for Paramount Network, with Yellowstone‘s fifth season premiere drawing in a record-breaking 12.1 million viewers and the final episode of 1923 attracting 7.4 million viewers. Both prequel series have been confirmed for second seasons, but premiere dates have yet to be announced.
