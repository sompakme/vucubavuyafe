Eiichiro Oda is a Japanese manga artist known for his iconic work, One Piece. For those who may not be familiar with the term manga, it is a style of Japanese comics and illustrated novels, for example: One Piece and Naruto

Oda’s signature One Piece is the best-selling manga in the history of the manga series. The work broke records in Japan, internationally and continues to do so to this day. We take a look at the genius behind this masterpiece.

Biography of Eiichiro Oda (Age)

Born in Kumamoto, Kumamoto Prefecture, Japan on January 1, 1975, at the age of 4, Eiichiro decided to become a manga artist and avoided any possibility of getting a “real job”. It’s fair to say that his dream turned out well. Oda throughout his life had inspirations that propelled him to where he is today. The pirates’ obvious interest in Oda and his work was sparked by the popular Animation TV series Vicky the Viking and his biggest influence in manga comes from Akira Toriyama and his series called Dragon Ball.

Oda started his journey to royal manga when he was a teenager, at the age of 17 to be precise. His first submitted work, Wanted! came in second place in the coveted Tezuka Award as well as winning other prizes. This success landed him a job at Weekly Shonen Jump magazine, initially working as Shinobu Kaitani’s series assistant. Police gang

At 19, Oda had started working with Nobuhiro Watsuki on Rurouni Kenshin. He had previously been with Mizu Tomodachi Kappaman and Masaya Tokuhiro the Tar-chan, King of the Jungle. Oda also drew two unique pirate-themed stories, titled Romance Dawn. Monkey D. Luffy, protagonist of Romance Dawn, would later play the same role in One Piece

After a few years of learning and improving his art style, in 1997 Oda started One Piece. It began serialization on Weekly Shonen Jump and evolved over the years to become the most popular manga in Japan and the best-selling manga of all time. One Piece tells the story of a young boy who wants to be the Pirate King and begins an endless journey with the Nakamas.

To give you an idea of ​​One Piece’s quality, let’s look at some numbers. By February 2005, One Piece had sold no less than 100 million collected tankōbon volumes. Fast forward to six years, the figure reaches over 200 million volumes. Its copies in print worldwide as of December 2014 amounted to 320,866,000 copies, and as of October 2017, 430 million volumes in circulation worldwide.

One Piece broke records for fun, continuously rising and breaking all previously set records on a regular basis. Volume 56 of One Piece sets the highest initial printing of any manga at 2.85 million copies. The next volume reached 3 million in 2010. One Piece won the 41st grand prize of the Japan Cartoonists Association Award in 2013.

Oda and his Manga friend Akira Toriyama created a crossover in 2007 that featured Toriyama’s One Piece and Dragon Ball characters. Crusader era. Dragon Ball holds the chalice of inspiration for One Piece. Eiichiro is very fond of One Piece fans who, even though he fell ill and was bedridden, made time to draw new chapters of One Piece. This was revealed by Masashi Kishimoto in 2013. Oda is a fan of rapper Eminem and is also a fan of singers Brook Benton, Izumi Katou, Avril Lavigne and Bryan Adams.

Apart from his involvement in manga creation, Eiichiro Oda is also involved in many charitable projects. In 2018, he donated $8 million to help Kumamoto Prefecture after the 2016 earthquake.

His wife (Chiaki Inaba) and children

Eiichiro Oda is married to a former model, Chiaki Inaba. Oda met Inaba when she was cosplaying Nami at Jump Fiesta performances in 2002.

The two started seeing each other and two years later, in 2004, the mangaka bonded over Inaba. The couple were blessed with a daughter in 2006.
